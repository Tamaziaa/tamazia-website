## HOW TO USE THIS LIST

**Design test:** For every section of the new site, ask: "If the Managing Partner of Clifford Chance, the Group CMO of Aman Resorts, or the General Counsel of Emaar landed here, would the site read as peer-grade or as vendor-grade?"

**Copy test:** Would Kirkland & Ellis pay £9,500/month based on this pitch? Would Julius Baer respond to this outreach email?

**Visual test:** Does this site look like it belongs next to patek.com, hermes.com, and cliffordchance.com when opened in adjacent browser tabs?

**If any of these tests fail at any section, that section is not yet done.**

## CLIENT-SIDE INSIGHT BY SECTOR (What each archetype actually cares about)

**Law firms (1-25):** SRA Transparency Rules, Bar Association advertising codes, directory visibility (Chambers, Legal 500, Chambers HNW), partner-level search dominance, crisis suppression, international visibility in key jurisdictions.

**Hotels (26-45):** Direct-booking revenue versus OTA dependency, Google Maps dominance, heritage property storytelling at scale, AI-search visibility for destination queries.

**Healthcare (46-60):** E-E-A-T authority on Google, HIPAA/MHRA/CQC alignment, before-and-after gallery compliance, patient journey content, crisis reputation management for named practitioners.

**Real Estate / IPO (61-75):** SEC Regulation FD, FCA financial promotion rules, pre-IPO digital footprint building, international HNW buyer targeting in multiple languages, virtual tour SEO.

**Finance (76-85):** FCA COBS 4, DFSA Conduct of Business, SEC Reg BI, whitepaper distribution, thought leadership ranking, confidential-grade engagement expectations.

**F&B (86-95):** Google Maps citations at scale, menu schema, reserve-a-table integration, review management, platform commission reduction (Deliveroo/Uber Eats).

**Education (96-97):** International parent targeting, admissions journey content, university ranking and authority, safeguarding compliance in published content.

**Professional services and institutional (98-100):** Peer-grade presence, no retail language, institutional references as the proof currency.
