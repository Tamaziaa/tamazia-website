# TAMAZIA · AESTHETIC REFERENCES
**17 curated sites. Study these before designing. Screenshots are in the `/screenshots/` folder.**
**Selection criterion: institutional luxury editorial + Swiss-watchmaker precision + magic-circle legal gravitas.**

The two references Aman specifically liked were Brunello Cucinelli and Clifford Chance. The other 15 below are calibrated to that mix.

---

## READING GUIDE

Each reference includes:
- **What it does well** (the specific design element Tamazia should learn from)
- **What to borrow** (the explicit carry-over for the Tamazia rebuild)
- **What NOT to copy** (traps to avoid)
- **Screenshot file**

## TIER 1 · LEGAL + ADVISORY GRAVITAS (6 references)

These sites set the institutional baseline. They prove the register Tamazia must speak.

### 01 · Clifford Chance
URL: https://www.cliffordchance.com/
Screenshot: `04-clifford-chance.png`

**What it does well.** Magic-circle legal authority. White background with conservative navy accent. Editorial serif for partner-grade presentation. Confident whitespace. Understated navigation. Content density without clutter.

**What to borrow.**
- The centred editorial wordmark and restrained header.
- Headline typography weight choice (medium serif, never bold display).
- Partner biography cards executed with whitespace as a luxury element.
- Grid discipline: every section respects a consistent column structure.
- Link styling: understated, no button-heavy clutter.

**What NOT to copy.** The blue accent (Tamazia uses oxblood + gold, not navy). The global-firm navigation maximalism (Tamazia is a boutique).

---

### 02 · Slaughter and May
URL: https://www.slaughterandmay.com/
Screenshot: `09-slaughter-may.png`

**What it does well.** The most conservative magic-circle firm. Monochrome with a single dark accent. Minimalism used as authority signal. Headlines in restrained serif. Navigation compressed to essentials.

**What to borrow.**
- Monochrome discipline at the highest level.
- Restrained navigation (7 or fewer items).
- Editorial weight in the partner cards.
- Silence as design element.

**What NOT to copy.** The near-total absence of colour (Tamazia's oxblood and gold need to be present, just restrained).

---

### 03 · Davis Polk & Wardwell
URL: https://www.davispolk.com/
Screenshot: `08-davis-polk.png`

**What it does well.** White-shoe US law firm. Clean hero typography. Case announcements treated editorially. Partner grid restrained. No stock imagery.

**What to borrow.**
- Case study panels inspired by their "Recent Matters" treatment.
- Left-aligned compositions with generous right-column whitespace.
- News feed styling for regulatory insights (future blog section).

---

### 04 · Macfarlanes
URL: https://www.macfarlanes.com/
Screenshot: `10-macfarlanes.png`

**What it does well.** Silver-circle UK firm. Subtle accent colour. Serif headlines with a quiet confidence. Testimonial-free approach.

**What to borrow.**
- Sector page treatments that feel like editorial insights, not sales pages.
- The absence of testimonials (matched by Tamazia's approach of using case studies, not testimonials).
- Hero composition: large serif + small supporting paragraph, no image in hero for many pages.

---

### 05 · Freshfields Bruckhaus Deringer
URL: https://www.freshfields.com/
Screenshot: `13-freshfields.png`

**What it does well.** Magic-circle with a more modern lean. Cleaner typography scale. Case announcements treated like FT stories.

**What to borrow.**
- Editorial headline-subheadline pacing.
- Consistent spacing system across pages (typographic rhythm).
- Thoughtful use of colour pops without overwhelming the page.

**What NOT to copy.** Any of the hero photography (Tamazia uses 3D objects, not photography).

---

### 06 · White & Case
URL: https://www.whitecase.com/
Screenshot: `12-white-case.png`

**What it does well.** Global elite firm. International reach signalled through typography and navigation, not through a map or "we work in X countries" gimmick.

**What to borrow.**
- Location strip at the top (parallel to Tamazia's dateline).
- Regulatory focus sections styled as editorial features.
- Partner bios that feel like FT columnist bylines.

## TIER 2 · BOUTIQUE ADVISORY + WEALTH (5 references)

Boutique investment banks and private wealth firms are the closest register to what Tamazia is: institutional-grade, boutique in scale, built on relationship capital.

### 07 · Perella Weinberg Partners
URL: https://www.pwpartners.com/
Screenshot: `07-perella-weinberg.png`

**What it does well.** Boutique IB. Editorial serif. Black + cream palette. Whitespace as luxury. Minimal navigation. No corporate clichés.

## TIER 3 · HERITAGE LUXURY + EDITORIAL CRAFT (6 references)

These sites set the craft ceiling. They prove what's achievable at the intersection of luxury and editorial rigour.

### 12 · Brunello Cucinelli
URL: https://www.brunellocucinelli.com/en/
Screenshot: `05-brunello-cucinelli.png`

**What it does well.** Italian heritage luxury. Cream + oxblood + gold palette, similar to Tamazia. Editorial photography. Serif typography with hierarchy. Whitespace generous. Craft signalled through detail.

**What to borrow (Aman specifically liked this).**
- The palette relationship (cream + burgundy + accent).
- Editorial long-form sections (Humanistic Capitalism page).
- Hero treatment that uses photography + typography together, not stacked.
- Footer restraint.

---

### 13 · Patek Philippe
URL: https://www.patek.com/en/home
Screenshot: `02-patek-philippe-v2.png` (v2 is the working version)

**What it does well.** Timepiece craft. Heritage gravitas. Full-bleed photography of timepieces treated like sacred objects. Navigation understated. Content paced like a luxury magazine.

**What to borrow.**
- Product (in our case, case study) presentation as sacred object.
- Centred single-object hero composition (direct reference for Tamazia's 3D centrepiece).
- Typography scale: small subline, large serif headline, pause, then body.

---

## HOW CLAUDE DESIGN USES THESE

When ingesting this document plus the screenshots, Claude Design should:
1. **Calibrate typography rhythm** from Perella Weinberg, Rothschild, Clifford Chance, FT.
2. **Calibrate palette relationships** from Brunello Cucinelli and the Tamazia logo.
3. **Calibrate composition and whitespace** from Aman Resorts, Patek Philippe, Assouline.
4. **Calibrate navigation restraint** from Slaughter and May, Stonehage Fleming.
5. **Calibrate section pacing** from FT, Christie's.

None of these sites is the template. Tamazia draws from multiple, synthesises its own voice, and passes the test: "would the Managing Partner of Clifford Chance, the CMO of Aman Resorts, and the Principal of Rothschild & Co all take this seriously."
