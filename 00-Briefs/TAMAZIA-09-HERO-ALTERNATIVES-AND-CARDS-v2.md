## PART 1 · FIVE NON-GLOBE HERO ALTERNATIVES

Ranked by how effectively they move a Managing Partner / Group CMO / General Counsel from the first three seconds on the page to booking a briefing.

### ALTERNATIVE 1 · THE LIVE NUMBER (RECOMMENDED #1)

**Concept:** The hero is a single verified statistic rendered in Freight Big Pro at display scale. `+96%` in gold, 320px tall, on obsidian. Below: `NYSE IPO share movement · CG Oncology (CGON) · Every word published was cleared against SEC disclosure.` In Inter 15px italic pearl. The H1 sits beneath: `Outrank competitors. Master regulators. One agency.` The CTA is immediately below the H1.

**Why it converts.** Evidence over claims. A listed-company General Counsel reading the FT at 6am sees a verified NYSE IPO number and immediately wants to know more. No illustration to interpret. No globe to decode. Just proof.

**Execution.** Requires CG Oncology written permission (which you'd want anyway). Zero 3D generation risk. Loads under 200ms because it is effectively typography. Easy Safari. Easy mobile.

**Risk.** Narrows the positioning to one case (healthcare IPO). Mitigated by rotating the featured stat on return visits (Kamat £1.2M saved one visit, CGON +96% next visit, Meraas metric third visit).

**Reference precedent.** Perella Weinberg's deal pages. Evercore's annual review. The Economist's data-as-hero covers.

**Conversion path.** See the number → read the H1 → click CTA → book briefing.

---

### ALTERNATIVE 2 · THE INSTANT AUDIT AS HERO (RECOMMENDED #2)

**Concept:** The Quick Audit Engine IS the hero. No separate section above it. The page opens with: pre-headline (founder credential line) + H1 + a single editorial sentence + the audit input box. Nothing else above the fold. Visitor lands, types domain, gets result in 2 seconds. The result card expands the page.

**Why it converts.** This gets the visitor to a value-exchange moment in under 10 seconds. Once a visitor has received a personalised finding, the psychological commitment to "see the full audit" is dramatically higher. Used well, this is the single highest-converting hero pattern for high-intent B2B.

**Execution.** Requires the Quick Audit backend built out (which is in the roadmap anyway). Risk of feeling SaaS-retail if visual register slips, but with the brand system applied (ivory, gold hairlines, Playfair italic, Freight Big Pro result metrics) it reads institutional, not retail. Think Bloomberg Terminal quick-look, not Hubspot CTA.

**Risk.** First impression is more functional than cinematic. Less "wow" at first glance. Compensated by the result card's visual weight once it appears.

**Reference precedent.** Linear's login / dashboard hero (institutional functional). FT Markets Alert. Bloomberg Terminal snippet widgets.

**Conversion path.** Type domain → see personalised report → click "Request full audit" → filled-in contact form → submit.

---

### ALTERNATIVE 3 · THE FOUNDER PORTRAIT EDITORIAL (RECOMMENDED #3)

**Concept:** A full-bleed editorial portrait of the founder (Aman) in formal attire, photographed in the register of an FT Weekend Magazine or Financial Times HTSI (How to Spend It) profile. Natural warm light, serious composition, not smiling. Positioned right 40%. Left 60% holds the H1, credentials, pull quote, and CTA. Caption under the portrait: `AMAN IGGA · LLM, INTERNATIONAL BUSINESS LAW · KING'S COLLEGE LONDON · FOUNDER, TAMAZIA · LONDON · DUBAI · NEW YORK · PARIS.`

**Why it converts.** For this clientele, the firm IS the founder. Peer audiences (managing partners, principals) hire individuals, not agencies. A confident editorial portrait immediately collapses the gap: "I can talk to this person." It radiates founder proximity, which is the single most powerful conversion signal for boutique professional services at this price point.

**Execution.** Requires one editorial-grade photograph (professional photographer, 1-2k USD, 2-hour session in Mumbai or London). This is a one-time investment. After that, the image is a permanent asset.

**Risk.** Requires the photograph to be genuinely good. A mediocre portrait is worse than no portrait. Use a photographer whose portfolio includes similar register (FT / Economist / Monocle contributors exist in India). Must feel editorial, not corporate headshot.

**Reference precedent.** FT HTSI founder profiles. Monocle business features. Brunello Cucinelli's founder portraits on their site. Perella Weinberg partner bio pages.

**Conversion path.** See portrait → trust the founder → click CTA → book briefing.

### ALTERNATIVE 4 · THE MANIFESTO (EDITORIAL TYPOGRAPHY ONLY)

**Concept:** No illustration at all. The hero is typographic manifesto territory. Full-bleed obsidian. Giant Playfair text emerges on load, one line at a time:

> `Every word you publish`
> `is a regulator's interview.`
>
> `We read it first.`

Below, a small credential line, then the H1 and CTA.

**Why it converts.** The strongest possible stake in the ground. Absolute restraint. Signals that the firm is so confident it needs no visual support. A Managing Partner who reads this feels addressed directly, not pitched.

**Execution.** Zero 3D, zero photography, zero asset production. Pure typography and motion. Fastest hero to build, fastest to load, easiest to maintain.

**Risk.** Typography-only requires the copy to be outstanding. The manifesto draft above is illustrative; final copy deserves three to five rounds of crafting. Also, some visitors may miss the visual anchor entirely on first glance.

**Reference precedent.** The Economist's editorial covers. Brunello Cucinelli's Humanistic Capitalism page. Berkshire Hathaway's annual shareholder letter (conceptually, not visually). Christie's lot description pages.

**Conversion path.** Read manifesto → agree → click CTA.

---

### ALTERNATIVE 5 · SINGLE-OBJECT OBSESSION (THE PATEK APPROACH)

**Concept:** One sculptural object, centred, massive whitespace around it. The original Scales of Law and Search rendered at gallery quality on obsidian with slow rotation. H1 positioned beneath, not beside. No globe, no constellation, no orbit — the object's quiet gravity is the whole statement.

**Why it converts.** For a specific subset of clientele (particularly luxury hospitality CMOs and heritage brand operators), restraint IS the signal. An audience that lives inside Patek, Hermès, and Aman Resorts responds to silence better than density. One object, treated with obsessive craft, says "we operate at your register."

**Execution.** Requires the single 3D object to be exceptional. This is a spotlight, so the object carries all the weight. If Claude Design can generate it cohesively, ship. If not, this alternative should be downgraded.

**Risk.** Single-case-study exposure plus single-object exposure is two points of failure. Either must be flawless. Also less immediate information density for visitors who scan rather than linger.

**Reference precedent.** Patek Philippe homepage. Rolls-Royce Spectre landing page. Aman Resorts destination pages. Hermès single-product focus pages.

**Conversion path.** See the object → sense the register → scroll → read H1 → click CTA.

## THE HYBRID THAT CONVERTS BEST (my strong recommendation)

Combine Alternatives 1 + 2 + 4 in layered form. The visitor never sees a wall of "options" — they experience one cohesive hero that moves them through three beats in under 15 seconds:

**Beat 1 (first 0.5 second)** · The Manifesto moment. Pre-headline Inter italic, then a single giant Playfair line like `Every word you publish is a regulator's interview.` The visitor feels addressed.

**Beat 2 (seconds 1-4)** · The H1 three-line stagger reveals underneath: `Outrank competitors. Master regulators. One agency.` Pull quote appears: `Ranking is only valuable if it is legal.` Credential strip below.

**Beat 3 (seconds 4-10)** · A single Live Number moment appears next to the H1, not hero-size but intimate: `+96%` in Freight Big Pro 120px gold with one line `NYSE IPO · CG ONCOLOGY (CGON) · ZERO COMPLIANCE INCIDENTS.` Subtle, not screaming.

**Beat 4 (seconds 10-30)** · Visitor scrolls one screen height. The Quick Audit Engine sits there. Personal data exchange available immediately.

This is the full conversion architecture. Each beat is quiet. Combined they do what no single hero element does alone.
