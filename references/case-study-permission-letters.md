# Case study permission letters · drafts

Three case studies on tamazia.co.uk reference identifiable third-party clients. Under UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f) (legitimate interest) and the case-study disclosure norms expected by sovereign wealth allocators, signed permission letters are the cleanest mitigation.

## A · Kamat Hotels India Ltd

Subject: Permission to publish case study · Tamazia × Orchid Hotels OTA reduction

To: [decision-maker name and title], Kamat Hotels India Ltd
From: Aman Pareek, Founder, Tamazia
CC: legal@kamathotels.com (or Kamat company secretary)

Dear [name],

I am writing to request the formal permission of Kamat Hotels India Ltd for Tamazia to continue to reference the Orchid Hotels engagement publicly on tamazia.co.uk and in subsequent investor or commercial materials, in the form attached.

The case study, which is published at https://tamazia.co.uk/insights/hotels/orchid-hotels-ota-83-percent/, describes the engagement scope, the methodology applied, and the outcome of the search-engine optimisation work that reduced OTA dependency for Orchid Hotels brand revenue by approximately 83 per cent. No commercially sensitive contractual terms are disclosed; revenue figures referenced are derived only from public Kamat Hotels investor materials and are linked to the published source.

Tamazia would be grateful if the attached one-page consent form could be signed and returned. The form is non-exclusive, perpetual, may be revoked on thirty days' written notice, and contains a paragraph by which Kamat Hotels expressly consents to Tamazia's continued reference to the Orchid Hotels brand for the limited purpose of describing this engagement to other prospective clients and investors.

If a different format of consent is preferred, or if specific edits to the published page would be helpful, please indicate and the page will be updated within forty-eight hours.

Yours sincerely,

Aman Pareek
Founder, Tamazia
LLM, King's College London (Business Law and International Arbitration)
